HELLO,

Beginner developer tips

A guide for navigating your entry to the world of software development

You start your journey in Days!

Preparing for your learning journey

  • Know your why. Congratulations on making the first step to becoming a Software Developer! Knowing the reason why you are choosing to go down this learning path to gain these valuable skills will keep you from burning out when it gets tough. Hold on to that why!
  • Make a plan. Stay in control of your learning outcomes by planning properly. Identify any blockers that may keep you from being successful these next several months and remove them before you begin your learning journey.
  • Master your tools. One of the biggest blockers a new software developer has is being unfamiliar with their computer and it's operating system. Get to know all of the tools you plan on working with while making software. This includes your web browser, the terminal, and any collaboration tools such as messaging platforms and GitHub.

During your learning journey

  • Rely on your peers. Not completely, but don't be an island. Your peers are your biggest asset and you are an asset to them. Plus who knows, you may be working alongside each other one day.
    "The best way to learn is to teach."
    — Frank Oppenheimer
  • Ask for help. If you are paying for help, use it! Don't let yourself get stuck on a problem for more than 15 minutes. Your instructors/tutors will be more than happy to help you and it's likely they've already solved the problem you are working through before. Don't be afraid of asking other developers you don't know for help, either. You may be surprised by the positive responses you'll get.
  • Learn how to research. Often times there will be problems that you run into where it is really vague what to search for in your search engines. Be resourceful. Keep a list of things you know, and things you don't know. Always be researching the things you don't know until you know them.

Overall tips for success

  • Silence is a setback. Engage with your peers. Celebrate your wins, talk about your struggles, and always ask questions. Try it, you won't regret it.
  • Embrace failure. There's going to be days that don't go as expected. That's just how it goes when learning something new. Reflect on your perceived failures and use them as a learning experience.
    “Success is most often achieved by those who don't know that failure is inevitable.”
    — Coco Chanel
  • Drink water. Seriously! Your brain composed of 73% water and it's going to be working overtime, requiring more hydration than usual. More importantly, look after your overall health. Take time for walks daily. Stretch during breaks. Get quality time with humans you love. Get enough sleep.
